Pakistan stock market likely to stay in red zone amid coronavirus fears

The global coronavirus outbreak hit the capital market badly as it witnessed the worst week in more than two and a half years and slumped by 5.6%, dealers said, expecting the situation to persist, reported The News on Sunday. “The coronavirus continued to keep a firm grip on local headlines, as the number of reported cases increased in Italy and neighbouring Iran, while a couple of cases were also identified in Pakistan,” Ahmed Lakhani, a JS Global analyst said.

Given the outbreak of the virus hitting more than four dozen countries with no signs of containment in sight global equities and commodities (such as oil) were quick to witness a rout. The panicky scenario was naturally not exclusive to Pakistan, with most major regional and global markets receding during the week. “Not in one session did the market close in the green zone, remaining firmly etched in red,” he said, according to the publication.
